Our Services

Existential Therapy

Life’s deeper questions can sometimes feel isolating: Why am I here? How do I live with purpose? Existential therapy offers space to reflect on meaning, freedom, and responsibility. By facing these questions together, you’ll gain clarity and courage to live in alignment with your values and authentic self.

Integrative Therapy

No two people are alike, and neither is healing. Integrative therapy draws from multiple approaches—mindfulness, DBT, IFS, person-centered, and more—to meet you where you are. Together we’ll build a flexible, holistic plan that supports your unique needs, strengths, and goals for growth.

Trauma Therapy

Past wounds can shape how we see ourselves and others. Trauma therapy provides a safe, supportive space to gently process these experiences without rushing or retraumatizing. We’ll focus on strengthening your inner resources, restoring safety in your body and mind, and moving toward a life worth living.

Hi, I’m Seth.

I believe that every person has an innate ability to heal, grow, and find meaning—even when life feels overwhelming or disorienting. I don’t see therapy as “fixing” people, but rather helping you to uncover your strengths, clarify your values, and create space for new possibilities.

I practice from an integrative and trauma-informed lens, drawing from modalities like IFS, DBT, existential therapy, interpersonal neurobiology, mindfulness, and person-centered counseling. This allows me to meet clients where they are—whether you’re seeking clarity on life’s biggest questions, relief from the weight of past trauma, or simply support navigating day-to-day stress. I work with individual adults and groups, and I hold a particular openness to neurodivergent clients who sometimes feel isolated or misunderstood.

What matters most to me is helping clients return to their true selves, reconnect with their inner resources, and step into lives marked by authenticity, courage, and purpose.

Outside of the therapy room, I enjoy woodworking as well as skiing, sailing, biking, and generally spending time outdoors. These practices inform my work, and they remind me that healing isn’t just about insight, but about moving down a path with heartfelt intention.

Best wishes,

Seth D. Muller, M.A., LPCC

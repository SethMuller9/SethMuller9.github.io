Frequently Asked Questions

Below are answers to common questions about therapy with New Pathways Psychotherapy. If you don’t see what you’re looking for, feel free to reach out.

Getting Started

What happens in the first session?

We’ll review your goals, history, and current concerns, then create an initial plan together. You’re welcome to share at your pace—there’s no pressure to cover everything at once.

How long are sessions and how often do we meet?

Standard sessions are 45–50 minutes, weekly or biweekly. Extended sessions (75–80 minutes) are available when clinically appropriate.

Do you offer telehealth?

Yes. Secure telehealth is available to clients located in Colorado. You’ll need a private space and a stable internet connection.

Who do you work with?

I provide individual therapy across a wide age range. I’m integrative and trauma-informed, and I especially enjoy working with meaning-seeking and neurodivergent clients who may feel isolated in high-demand environments.

Approach & Fit

What approaches do you use?

Integrative and person-centered care drawing from modalities such as mindfulness, IFS-informed work, DBT skills, Narrative Therapy, existential, and strengths-based therapy. We’ll tailor the work to your goals and preferences.

Is therapy right for me if I’m not sure what I need?

Absolutely. Many people start with a sense that “something needs to change.” We’ll clarify goals together and take it step by step.

Can you help with past trauma?

Yes. We’ll proceed safely and collaboratively, focusing on stabilization, choice, and pacing.

Fees & Insurance

Do you take insurance?

I’m currently out-of-network and do not bill insurance directly. Upon request, I can provide a superbill for you to submit to your insurer for potential out-of-network reimbursement.

Do you offer a sliding scale?

Yes. A limited number of reduced-fee spots are available based on household income and size, guided by Federal Poverty Guidelines. See the Fees & Insurance page for details.

What is your cancellation policy?

Please provide 24-hour notice to cancel or reschedule. Late-cancel/no-shows may be billed up to 50% of the full session fee, except in emergencies at my discretion.

Privacy & Safety

Is therapy confidential?

Yes, with important exceptions required by law and professional ethics (e.g., risk of harm, suspected abuse/neglect, court orders). We’ll review informed consent and limits of confidentiality in the first session.

What if I’m in crisis?

My therapy services are by appointment only. This website and email are not for emergencies. If you’re in immediate danger, call 911 or 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline). In Colorado, call or text 844-493-8255 (Colorado Crisis Services).

Administrative

Do you write letters or complete forms?

Brief coordination is part of care. Extensive letters, forms, or reports may be billed in 15-minute increments at your session rate unless otherwise arranged.

Will you go to court on my behalf?

While I can provide forensic evaluations or expert testimony, if I’m compelled to participate in legal matters (e.g., subpoenas, depositions, or court appearances), you’re responsible for related costs, including reasonable attorneys’ fees, preparation, and appearance time. See the Fees & Insurance page.

How do I get started?

Reach out via the contact form or email to request a brief consultation. We’ll discuss your goals, availability, and next steps.

Integrative Therapy

An integrative approach means I pull from several evidence‑based modalities—IFS, person‑centered, DBT skills, and somatic & existential work—so your care fits you, not the other way around.

Who this helps

  • High‑achieving or neurodivergent adults who feel stuck in patterns that no longer serve them
  • People navigating anxiety, trauma responses, grief, or big transitions
  • Clients seeking practical tools and deeper meaning—not one without the other

How sessions work

Warm, structured, and collaborative. We’ll set goals, notice what’s happening in your body, and use targeted methods without losing the human connection that makes therapy work.


  • IFS (Internal Family Systems): Befriend protective parts and heal wounded parts to reduce reactivity and shame.
  • DBT skills: Emotion regulation, distress tolerance, and interpersonal effectiveness you can use right away.
  • Somatic awareness: Gentle nervous‑system regulation to increase safety and choice.
  • Existential lens: Clarify values, purpose, and responsibility so growth lasts.

What you can expect

  • Less overwhelm and more capacity to respond (not just react)
  • Clearer boundaries and more satisfying relationships
  • Momentum toward a life aligned with your values
